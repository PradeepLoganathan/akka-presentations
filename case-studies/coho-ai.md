## The challenge

Coho AI set out to launch a product-led revenue-optimization platform with limited time and a small engineering team. The company planned to scale to dozens of customers and thousands of users within months of launch, which required a production-grade system, not a prototype.

Building that foundation the conventional way meant provisioning cloud infrastructure, writing operational boilerplate, and standing up the resilience and scaling machinery before any product work began. The internal estimate for that path was **four to six months** — time a startup competing for its first customers did not have.

## Why Akka

Coho AI chose **Akka Specify**: it defined the platform it needed, and a governed production system was delivered and operated on its behalf, running cloud-native services on AWS. The team wrote product logic instead of infrastructure code, and the system it launched on was the system it kept running as customers arrived.

This is the **delivered-outcome** path: specifications go in, and a governed production system comes out in weeks, fully operated — with clustering, resilience, durable state, and scaling handled so the application does not have to carry them.

## The results

**Scale.** Coho AI grew from launch to dozens of paying customers and thousands of users within months, absorbing that growth on the same delivered system with no re-platforming.

**Cost to operate.** Operating overhead ran 60% lower. With no infrastructure to provision and no operational boilerplate to maintain, the company launched and ran the platform without dedicated operations hires.

**Speed to production.** Time to market improved 75%. A build estimated at four to six months shipped in **two months**, delivered and operated end to end.

**Business impact.** "With Akka, we got to market 75% faster compared to other solutions we had considered," said Michael Ehrlich, Co-founder and CTO of Coho AI. The company converted that speed into paying customers in its first months.
