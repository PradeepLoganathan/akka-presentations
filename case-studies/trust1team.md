## The challenge

Trust1Team delivers custom software for large organizations across fintech, insurance, healthcare, government, and IoT. Every engagement carries the same weight of non-functional requirements — security, reliability, persistent state, and operational resilience — that a client expects in production but that consume delivery time before any business feature is written.

The pattern held across the work: 60–80% of a typical project is CRUD and integration plumbing. Building that plumbing by hand on each new system limited a three-person team to roughly **one microservice per sprint**, and slowed delivery of the diagnostics, monitoring, and transaction systems clients actually paid for — energy-usage benchmarking, 24/7 wearable health monitoring for elderly care, and point-of-sale and booking portals for indoor ski centers.

## Why Akka

Trust1Team standardized on Akka because it removes the repetitive engineering that dominates delivery. Persistence, clustering, resilience, and durable state are provided by the platform, so the team defines the data model and cost-attribution logic and gets a scalable, secure service without rebuilding the foundations each time.

Michallis Pashidis, Founder and CEO of Trust1Team, stated the firm is confident Akka will increase developer productivity and deliver 3x acceleration in real-world delivery. The firm has run that bet across 40+ projects over a decade, including two years transforming the ski-center systems.

## The results

**Scale.** Trust1Team's Akka systems process 10–15 million invoices a year and span 40+ delivered projects — IoT energy diagnostics, wearable health monitoring streaming geolocation, accelerometer, and heart-rate data with 24/7 alerts, and redesigned point-of-sale, vending, and online booking systems.

**Cost to operate.** With the 60–80% of each project that is CRUD and plumbing handled by the platform, operational overhead drops and a small team maintains far more system per engineer.

**Speed to production.** Development runs 3x faster: a three-person team ships three microservices per sprint where it previously shipped one.

**Business impact.** The firm sustains delivery across insurance, healthcare, government, and fintech clients with the same small teams, and has done so for ten years on the platform.

## The agentic opportunity

Trust1Team's work is the natural shape of spec-driven delivery. A client brings a specification — an energy-diagnostics model, an invoice-reconciliation flow, an eligibility and fraud check across millions of transactions — and expects a governed production system, not a research project. Through **Akka Specify**, those specifications go in and a running, fully operated system comes out in weeks: an AI agent that ingests invoices, reconciles line items, runs fraud and eligibility checks, and escalates exceptions to a human, with durable state that survives failure and an audit trail on every decision. The 60–80% that is CRUD and plumbing is generated and governed; the delivery team defines the business logic and the guardrails.
