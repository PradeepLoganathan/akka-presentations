Workforce Adoption

Scaling AI starts with principles.

Before scaling tools, scale judgment.

  1. 01Use data and AI aligned to our Code of Business Conduct and Trust Center policies.
  2. 02Prioritize customer, colleague, and organizational safety through governance-first practices.
  3. 03Assume AI tools become the primary interface for employees, partners, and customers.
  4. 04Enable employee and partner agency to take risks, move faster, and build skill.
  5. 05Create transparency and acceptance as roles become more fluid.
  6. 06Learn from the controlled injuries that emerge from autonomy.
  7. 07Build an AI-first competency we can bring to the market.
  8. 08Invest in enablement and training — fluency is a skill, and judgment under AI must be taught, not assumed. It’s how we get the most from these tools and avoid laziness and fatigue.
Adoption Reality

AI makes people faster.
It can also make them slower.

The adoption challenge is preserving judgment under acceleration.

Leverage
  • Faster synthesis
  • Pattern detection
  • Ambiguity compression
  • Draft generation

Productivity gains are real when AI is applied to the right work.

Friction
  • AI laziness — polished output, light review
  • AI fatigue — more prompting and checking
  • Review debt — savings spent verifying intent
  • Workslop — complete-looking, low-value output
AI does not remove judgment. It raises the premium on judgment.

Akka Governance Platform
Evaluation is not control.
€35M Penalties
174 regulatory frameworks — all of them changing. Which ones apply to your agents? How do you re-posture every time they shift? EU AI Act penalties alone reach €35M.
Outside Looking In
Governance tools observe AI from the outside. They can't enforce at agent boundaries, can't gate a deployment, can't link a policy decision to the human who approved it. They produce reports — not proof.
PB+
Petabyte-scale storage for regulatory holds — 10-year retention for high-risk AI under EU AI Act Art. 72. Copied logs aren't tamper-proof. When auditors come, you need hash-chains — not hope. Governance isn't cheap; it has to be built in.
The control loop
Akka's governance is a runtime control loop: Classify, Approve, Enforce, Prove.
Classify
Map each AI system to 174 frameworks and 521 controls to derive its obligation set.
Approve
Route dossiers through Legal, Risk, Security, Model Risk, and business-owner sign-off recipes.
Enforce
Apply guardrails, eval checkpoints, HITL gates, policy decisions, and containment inline.
Prove
Emit non-sampled interaction logs, evidence bundles, legal holds, retention state, and audit records.
Runtime safety
Policies stay current as regulations change.
Unsafe actions are stopped before execution, not scored later.
Every decision has a runtime evidence trail.
Separate AI eval and observability tools become optional, not required.
