                    Identify your AI controls

                    Your Risk Survey answers compile into the Eval Matrix — one signed contract listing every safeguard your system must implement and prove.                     From regulations Auto-derived from the corpus of 962 controls across 183 frameworks worldwide, using your survey answers.         .updateState(state.pauseFor("licensed-underwriter"))

                    Governance is a feedback and improvement loop

                    Systems change. Models drift. Regulations move. The evidence you signed six months ago may no longer reflect reality. Akka watches for the changes that matter and scopes the review to what actually changed — and the same evaluation, testing, and replay techniques also drive continuous improvement of the system's safety and accuracy.

                    You declare a change

                    A prompt edit, a model swap, a new tool, a new country, a retrain on fresh data — one of nineteen named change types from the AI events taxonomy.

                    A regulation changes

                    A new regulation publishes, an existing article amends, a framework deprecates. Akka's monthly corpus watch detects the change and propagates it to every affected system.

                    A scheduled review comes due

                    Annual recertification, third-party audit, attestation renewal. Akka tracks the dates and tells you what is coming up before the deadline arrives.

                    The runtime detects drift

                    A guardrail tripping more often than its threshold, an approval rate shifting, a vendor model behaving differently. The platform raises an operational event and asks whether a change event should be declared.

                    Each AI change event determines the risk review and testing required. A configuration tweak is not the same risk as a foundation-model swap. Akka generates the data sets and test harnesses you need from production traces.

                    A typical year

                    Posture improvement is not a quarterly fire drill. It is a steady cadence of small, scoped cycles — some triggered by a change event, some driven by your own evaluation work to sharpen the system's safety and accuracy. Here is what a year looks like for a regulated AI system in production:

                    WhenWhat runs
                    Day 0Steps 1, 2, 3 in full. New system. Several days to weeks of front-loaded work.
                    Day 14Step 4 fires: prompt tuned. T2 — quick replay over recent traffic, targeted sign-off from two roles. Half-day cycle.
                    Day 45Step 4 fires: new tool added. T2 — quick replay, the model-risk owner re-signs. Day's work.
                    Day 90Step 4 fires: model retrained. T1 — sandboxed replay over ninety days, shadow run for seven days, safety red team scheduled, full sign-off chain. Multi-week cycle but pre-determined and pre-budgeted.
                    Day 120Step 4 fires: corpus update — a new article in the European Union Artificial Intelligence Act amends Article 50. T4 — interpretation notes added to affected controls, targeted re-sign-off on the transparency section only.
                    Day 365Step 4 fires: periodic annual recertification. T3 — replay sweeps for undeclared drift, coverage check confirms nothing is unserviceable, an attestation refresh.

                    The loop is not friction. It is the price of operating an AI system in a regulated environment, and it is dramatically cheaper when the platform pre-determines the scope of each cycle rather than negotiating it bespoke with auditors each time.
