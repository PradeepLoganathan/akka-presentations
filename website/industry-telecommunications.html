Why telecom is different

Peak is the normal state.

A device launch or holiday sale can multiply traffic in minutes. Provisioning, billing, and network assurance run continuously and cannot pause. Telecom systems have to scale to the spike, stay up through failure, and do it without a costly parallel-site rebuild every year.

  • Traffic is spiky. Launch-day and seasonal peaks arrive fast and can't be under-provisioned for.
  • Always-on. Provisioning, billing, and assurance run 24/7 — downtime is lost revenue and churn.
  • Cost pressure is constant. Growing volume can't mean linearly growing infrastructure.
  • Legacy is the incumbent. Decade-old commerce and OSS/BSS stacks are the systems being replaced.

Control built into every agent action.

The same platform that carries telecom's highest-volume traffic keeps the agents on it safe and accountable — a real-time check on every action, and a record that holds up.

Guardrails

Block unsafe outputs

Runtime filters catch prohibited or unsafe content before an agent can act on it.

Sanitizers

Strip sensitive data

Personal and confidential data is automatically removed from what agents read and write.

Evaluations

Checked in real time

Every agent action is checked inline as it happens — pass, block, or flag — so an unsafe action never executes.
